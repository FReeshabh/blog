Name of the Project: Labyrinth Escape VR

Abstract Idea: Labyrinth Escape VR is a two-player maze puzzle explorer game. One player is the maze runner who traverses the maze in a VR world. The other player is a teammate with a navigational map that helps the maze runner escape the labyrinth using hints and clues that the navigator gets using his information map. This two-person puzzle brings a new look to escape games everywhere. This game is not designed to take a long time to complete but instead focuses on teamwork and improving critical thinking. We expect players to take no longer than 10-20 minutes to complete the game using our current design.

Purpose for project:

  • Audience

Our target audience mainly focuses on Children.This can also be played between a child and a parent. We recommend that the parent always plays as the navigator. We believe our game will help stimulate and improve a child's intellectual capacity. Not only do we want to focus on a child’s ability to improve their education but teach them team work as well. In doing this we are attempting to steer away from typical single player based games and create a VR environment that has been specifically designed for two players to help improve the intellectual capacity of the players as well as help them discover how important teamwork is in not only the game but real life.

  • Education

Riddles have been used to “work [a person's] brain” (Justin Zablocki). Using riddles stimulates a person’s intellectual capacity and makes them work with critical thinking and problem solving. Riddles also can help improve “reading comprehension” and “vocabulary” (Justin Zablocki). Riddles accomplish this by making players use context clues and critically reading to allow them to solve the problem. In this game, we are focusing on an easier set of problems to allow us to develop and create this interactive two player environment. However, we could easily increase the difficulty of the intersection riddles to create a more rigorous course to be used by adults or previous players who want more of a challenge.

  • Teamwork

Another aspect we wanted to focus on was teamwork. Our game creates a labyrinth that can only be solved if both parties work together. Though the navigator will receive the riddle or part of the riddle, the runner will be the only one able to see the intersection. This means they must work together in order to solve the puzzle and choose the next path.

  • Communication

Communication will also be key when solving the labyrinth. We added some twists to some riddles to make the runner and navigator need to communicate in order to solve a puzzle. This not only helps with problem solving but allows the two players to figure out proper communication and use it to solve each intersection.

Initial design:

  • Number of Players and Type:

There is currently a minimum and maximum of two players in order to correctly solve this puzzle. One player will be given a map which will periodically give him clues while the other player will be in the maze trying to escape by communicating to each other.

        Maze Runner: The maze runner will be the player who is inside the maze. He

        must convey what he sees to the navigator as they try to solve a puzzle together.

He will have the ability to look around and make a choice at each intersection. He will also have to trust his navigator to convey the riddles and clues correctly in order to figure out each correct path.

Navigator: The navigator will be the player with the map. The map will periodically give a new riddle with each intersection the player comes along. Based on these riddles, and the maze runners clues and hints. The two can communicate to figure out the correct path to take so they can complete the labyrinth.

        

  • Maze:

Description: The maze will have multiple correct paths. We did this for two main reasons. The first reason is that there may be a few super easy riddles with multiple correct paths in order to get the hang of the game. The second reason is so that if you do make a mistake and go down a right path and there is still a way to escape, you may still win the labyrinth. This acts more as an unseen extra life in some areas. The maze will be decorated and at each intersection, decorations, such as objects, wall art, and floor art, may give you a clue to figure out which way to proceed based on the navigator's riddle.

Design: 

When making the maze I initially created a couple of cubes to serve as the plain and the player and the walls.

I gradually added more walls to create the feeling of a maze. Things got really complex.

 I started adding diagonals and small corridors to create a sense of claustrophobia reminiscent of the horror game we had initially wanted to make.

I then tested it out by adding a simple movement script and position the camera as a child of the player cube to get a feel for what it would look like if you were in the maze and I was impressed with the results.

I then realized I needed to make the maze simpler because we were talking about telling the game via voice controls, where to go/ which path to take and we needed to have predetermined paths. We also wanted to implement an enemy npc that could find the player and in order to do that reliably, we needed to create a simpler maze with only left and right being the available movement choices( or at least ones that we could feasibly pull off).

Maze version 2 was one in which I made the paths first and I planned to fill in the walls later. I soon came to realize this would be a daunting amount of work to add the walls after I had began. I also implemented(and we have this video in our references) what is called a “Nav Mesh” using assets online which allow for the player character to move in the direction you were facing. Its based on raycast and essentially is a move where clicked script, however it is always on, and the NavMesh needs to be pre-baked before running the code.

After realizing the difficulty in going back over this maze, I got to work on finding a maze generating algorithm online, and implementing that and editing the pieces to create a maze to my liking (this is also included in our references). The name of the maze algorithm is “hunt and kill” because it hunts for usable paths. The trouble with this type of maze was it only had one path.

We wanted to include multiple path and the following image is my attempt at highlight which paths were removed.

                

Later I got the idea to include several , perhaps 2x2 mazes and had them connect to each other, so that I could get  the path location more reliably. The way we do this is to make a copy of whatever maze the algorithm generates and make it into one of our prefabs. We can then reuse this asset. I used this process to create the entire 16x16 maze and then I added tiles that corresponded to the correct answers and wear we could ask our trivia question. I highlighted colors for each path to show we had 6 possible ways a player could go. I kept iterating on this until there were plenty of alternate paths. Through a process of counting I determined a way that no matter which path the player takes, they have to answer 5 questions. There are a total of 16 yellow squares which will represent the rooms we will implement which initiate questions. Since the maze itself is kind of narrow and cramped, ideally we would want the player to get teleported to another room.

Finally I had the idea to link every since yellow square to each other through links that allow the player to jump from intersection to intersection, skipping the long and boring walking time, and this drastically improved the amount of fun I was having while running through the maze. There is a slight vertical change that really gives the player a sense that they are hopping from place to place.

                

        

  • Map:

Description: The map will be a minimap of the maze. However there will be fog of war in place. This means the navigator cannot see the full maze and tell the runner how to escape without using the riddles. The map will have three important components. The first component will be a side panel to the left of the map. This panel is where a riddle will appear when a player arrives at an intersection. The second component would be a green dot on the minimap that indicated the current location of the player in the maze. Finally, the third component would be a red dot that can be seen through fog of war. This red dot indicates a barbarian. Players may have to hide from a barbarian if it approaches them and there is still a way out of the labyrinth. If there is not a way out, meaning the player already lost. The barbarians will hunt him and he will not be able to hide ending the game.

Design: (Environment): We are going for a sort of old school elder scrolls vibe but with modern graphics and vr as the player navigates the maze. I want the player to feel impressed and surprised as they see each room and notice how much detail was added in every scene. The walls should be brick but they also have candle lights and torches, a library and a bookcase, a fireplace, blood, skeletons, evidence of life. There should be particles, like smoke rising, sand and water falling, lighting that changes based on the temperature of the room. When the player has to answer a riddle I want the room to really have a sense of magic to it, like they actually feel like they have been transported to a strange room and that they have to answer the riddle for their life. We want to include sound effects like water dripping or a creaking noise, the sound of a screaming barbarian as he comes near. There should be unique items all over the maze that act as hints, a broken window that lets you see the other side, an escape ladder, spikes shoot out immediately after the player leaves the spot to give a thrilling sense of adventure. We are going for photo realism.

A boulder running after the player as they run towards the final goal.In addition we also hope to include small minigames and intractable objects for the player immersion.

(Minimap): There will be a minimap that the player 2, the navigator, will see which displays a simplified version of the maze with a “fog of war” added which basically shows only what is near the player and where they’ve been. We still need to implement this but, we are working on a script that is able to detect the next available paths in this fog of war. The visual information that the runner sees is then translated into this simplified 2D image that portrays fog of war similarly, but not exactly like, RTS games. Alternatively we can also just make the Minimap an orthographic camera.

  • Barbarian

Description: The barbarian will be the only NPC (non player character) that can actually end the players quest to beat the labyrinth. The barbarian will move randomly throughout the maze and if a player is on a correct path and a barbarian approaches the player may hide until it leaves. However, if the player is not on a correct path and there are no other ways the player may beat the labyrinth, the barbarian will then hunt the player taking direct paths, and the player will not be able to hide. This will result in the game ending.

  • Movement:

Description: The runner will be able to move through the maze and jump from intersection to intersection allowing him to decide which path to take next based on the riddle discussion between him and the navigator. The player will also be allowed to use a voice command to switch between movement mode and camera mode allowing him to freely look around.

Design: Several methods of movement were created for different use cases. Several scripts were created. For example on PC the uses has the choice to move with a mouse to the spot they are facing. They can also press the escape key to remove cursor lock and interact with the game’s other options like “free camera” which enables them to take stalk of their environment. In VR, we hope to be able to allow the player to be able to say “camera” and the game automatically recognizes to stop the player from moving and look around until they are ready.

The current UI is essentially for testing purposes but we can later include a UI similar to this but it can be gazed based. There is also a WASD based movement option. In the future, we hope to implement OpenVR support to enable a mode where a player with joysticks can play. So we have CardBoard and PC support currently but not high end VR just yet.

I also made it to wear, instead of running to just where the player looks, they instead jump to the next intersection to save time. I will later make this option player selectable in an options menu that is gazed based.

We hope to innovate dramatically in the movement arena. For example, if the entire movement system is gaze based like we currently have it, if we create a speed variable, and get data from wireless VR controllers that one of us has, then we can get data for how fast they are waving their hands up and down. If we are able to record how fast they are moving their arms up and down then we will be able move the player faster (as if they were running).

  • Voice:

Description: We have decided to incorporate a few voice commands to add to the uniqueness of our VR environment. Below will be a list of these commands and what they do:

  • Camera

In camera mode the runner will be able to freely look around and survey each intersection.

  • Movement

In movement mode the runner will be able to jump from one intersection to the next. However we do not allow the maze runner to backtrack.

  • Hide

When the runner says hide, he will hide in a box or a barrel. This is used to avoid being killed by a randomly passing barbarian. This will not work on a hunting barbarian.

  • Leave

When the runner says leave, he will exit the box or barrel and may continue his quest.

  • Menu

When a player says menu he will be given instructions for the game. As well as described how movement works and other mechanics of the game. There will be something in the beginning of the game that says please say menu. This will encourage the runner to pull up the menu screen and see how to navigate and hide.
